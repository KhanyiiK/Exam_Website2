Exploring Near-Earth Objects (NEOs): Fascinating Facts about Asteroids and Comets

Asteroids, also known as Near-Earth Objects (NEOs), are rocky objects that orbit the Sun and can be found in various regions of the solar system.

  • NEO Definition:

    NEOs are asteroids and comets that have been nudged by the gravitational attraction of nearby planets into orbits that bring them into close proximity to Earth.

  • Size Variation:

    NEOs come in various sizes, ranging from a few meters to hundreds of kilometers in diameter. The vast majority of NEOs are relatively small, but even small ones can have a significant impact if they collide with Earth.

  • Discovery and Tracking:

    Astronomers use ground-based and space-based telescopes to discover, track, and monitor NEOs. Several observatories around the world contribute to the NEO tracking efforts.

Studying NEOs is essential for understanding the potential impact risk they pose and for developing strategies to mitigate this risk, as well as for learning more about the early solar system's formation and evolution.
